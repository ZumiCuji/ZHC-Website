Soil Moisture Assessment & Response Tracking Satellite

  • LinkedIn
  • Email

S.M.A.R.T. was designed and developed by a team of 7 aerospace engineers at North Carolina State University. As project manager, I led those engineers through the entire process. A detailed set of functional requirements laid the foundation for the satellite. The satellite was built with a very limited budget. The final price tag was around $1200. This trailblazed cheap satellite development. The satellite was created in response to devastating flooding in western North Carolina. S.M.A.R.T. was able to use synthetic aperture radar to detect objects hundreds of meters away. This data was then sent wirelessly to a ground station which then developed a map.
